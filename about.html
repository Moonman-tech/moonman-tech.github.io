About Scrape

Scrape is a South African website that was created to be a hub for meaningful South African media content, a hub for investment requests, small businesses exposure, local sports updates, agriculture information and education from farmers, local music, cultural awareness and South African tourism sites.

Many people argued that local sports also deserves publicity because athletes start somewhere before participating on the bigger stage. There are huge sporting leagues withing local sports that deserves publicity to give young athletes a chance to be recognized nationally for their efforts. Women sporting events do not receive as much coverage as men's sporting events and we believe in equality. Broadcasting the sporting events might be costly to the SABC and making it almost impossible to broadcast many sporting events. We want to give athletes as much publicity as we can because regardless of which sport is the most popular in our country, the youth need to partake in any sporting event that they might be interested in and they must easily have access to it. For that to happen, they will have to get the countries support and have private companies or the government sponsor sporting facilities needed by the youth or the communities. In the sports section, we will also be working to promote healthy living to strengthen our people's immune systems and mental well-being.

There are tons of fashion designers in South Africa with skills to perform on the bigger scale. Proudly South African fashion designers were able to dress top American celebrities and if you take a drive or walk around CBD's in South Africa, you will see the potential and skills that fashion designers have across the country. South African local fashion designers are often taken for granted because they dwarfed by foreign brands making it extremely hard to be recognized or given a chance to prove themselves. We want to create a space for local fashion designers to showcase their skills and work. The aim is to promote local fashion designer and get them to produce large South African owned brands.

South Africa has been making a lot of money from the tourism industry for decades but that trend was stopped by lockdowns. There are many places in South Africa that can attract tourists from all around the world, besides our history we have dozens of tourist attractions in in each on the nine provinces in South Africa. There are many famous tourist attractions and there are even more attractions that are yet to be visited. The tourism industry created many job opportunities and promoting our tourism attractions will invite foreign currencies to our country meaning the economy will see growth. We need to promote domestic tourism because many people believe that South Africa does not have much to offer as domestic tourism is not being marketed or promoted to a greater extent. We want to help domestic tourism and help create job opportunities and grow our tourism sector.

Sharing a picture of cuisines from all around South Africa is another method of promoting South African tourism. South Africa is known for diversity, we have more than many African countries and we try by all means be united and embrace the culture of our fellow South Africans. Every culture has its own cultural cuisines and some share the same cuisines, touring across the country will give tourists chances to taste the cuisines belonging to different cultures. In South Africa we have many qualified chefs that are trying to prove themselves by opening their own restaurant but they do not have the support of their community because large franchises are dwarfing their restaurants and taking away all their customers. As it is said in the above paragraph, we need to create South African owned for South Africans and expand to other countries. Posting the cuisine pictures will show people what they are missing by not touring and it should bring customers from all around the country (of not the world) to South African restaurants.

Many small and medium South African businesses and companies lost a huge amount of money and some were forced to close during lockdown, financial assistance is essential at this point for businesses. Our goal is to help businesses recover and maybe open other new businesses. We are creating a space for businesses to make presentations as investment requests for their businesses. Getting investments will help businesses recover quicker and selling shares of a business will welcome another brain on to the table and that should make growing your business easier and quicker. We think businesses need to recover quick and we can reach that goal by working together, what do you think?

The agricultural sector has been contributing well to the economy for decades and it is known for being very profitable but only a few numbers of people are active in that sector. There are many reasons why people are not participating in the agricultural sector, lack of skills, lack of patience and lack of funding is some of the reasons why there few people involved in the agricultural sector. We will be giving out information that could encourage people see the good side of agriculture or even be influenced by other people's success in the agriculture sector. We will be sharing stories and tips from real farmers with the aim of educating viewers.

We want to create a new South Africa where poverty is low and people are less dependent on the government, politics have failed us many times, we are trying to not depend on the government. We believe we can change our country if we work smarter and harder, we have billions on information on our fingertips and we can use that to our advantage and change the country for the better.
